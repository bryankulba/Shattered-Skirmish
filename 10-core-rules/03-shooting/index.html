    Shooting

    The Shooting Phase resolves all ranged attacks made by eligible models.

    All shooting is resolved at the model level.

    There is no wound pooling and no unit-based targeting.

    1. Eligibility to Shoot

    A model may shoot if:

    • It did not Run this activation.
    • It did not Charge this activation.
    • It is not Engaged in close combat.

    Assault weapons may always fire unless explicitly prohibited by another rule.

    2. Targeting

    • A model must target the closest visible enemy model where applicable.
    • Line of sight must be physically traceable from the firing model to the target.
    • Cover and visibility modifiers follow Mordheim conventions.

    All attacks are resolved against a single target model.

    3. Shooting Modifiers

    The following modifiers apply:

    • –1 to hit if the firing model moved and is using a non-Assault weapon.
    • –1 to hit if the target is beyond half of the weapon’s maximum range.

    Modifiers stack unless explicitly stated otherwise.

    Cover Modifiers

    Cover represents terrain or obstacles that partially obscure a target from incoming fire. If a target is partially hidden by terrain, the shooter suffers a –1 penalty to hit (soft cover). If the target is mostly hidden behind solid terrain, the penalty is –2 to hit (hard cover).

    If a shot passes within 1" of another model, the target counts as being in soft cover (–1 to hit).

    A model that cannot be seen at all cannot be targeted.

    4. Resolving a Shot

    To resolve a ranged attack:

    1. Declare target.
    2. Roll To Hit (BS vs required value).
    3. Roll To Wound.
    4. Resolve Armour Saves.
    5. If an unsaved wound is caused, roll on the Injury table.

    Critical hits bypass armour saves.

    Injury resolution is defined in:

    04-melee.md (Injury Resolution section)

    5. Ammunition & Scarcity

    In SHATTERED, ammunition is limited, so every shot matters and running out can cost you the fight. All ranged and special weapons are subject to limited amounts of ammunition each game.

    Weapons with a Firepower (FP) characteristic greater than 2 still count as a single shot.

    Bolt Pistols are not subject to ammunition scarcity.

    5.1 Ranged Weapon (Boltguns) Ammunition

    At the start of the battle, roll 1D6 per ranged weapon (Boltguns) to determine ammunition availability.

    The result applies for the duration of that battle only.

    D6 Result
    1 Unstable Supply – Any unmodified hit roll of 1 triggers a Weapon Misfire roll.
    2–5 The weapon may fire that many times during the battle.
    6 Unlimited shots for the battle.

    5.2 Special Weapon Ammunition

    At the start of the battle, roll 1D3 per ranged weapon to determine ammunition availability.

    The result applies for the duration of that battle only.

    D6 Result
    1 One shot during the battle.
    2 Two shots during the battle.
    3 Three shots during the battle.

    6. Special Weapons – Risk of Catastrophic Failure

    All special weapons are unstable.

    Any unmodified hit roll of 1 made with a special weapon immediately triggers a roll on the Weapon Misfire Table, regardless of ammunition availability.

    This effect is in addition to any misfire triggered by an Unstable Supply result.

    7. Weapon Misfire Table

    When a weapon suffers a misfire, roll 1D6:

    D6 Result
    1 BOOM! The weapon explodes. The shooter suffers an S4 hit (no critical hit possible). The weapon is destroyed.
    2 Jammed. The weapon is jammed and useless for the remainder of the battle.
    3 Phut. The weapon fails to fire. The shooter must wait one extra turn before firing this weapon again.
    4–5 Click. The weapon fails to fire with no additional effect.
    6 KA-BOOM! The shot hits the intended target and counts as having +1 Strength.

    Destroyed weapons may not be used again during the battle.

    Persistent damage effects are resolved in the Campaign Rules.
